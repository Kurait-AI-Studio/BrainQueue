BrainQueue — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 11 June 2026

BrainQueue (“we”, “the app”) is a personal task manager. This policy explains what data we handle, why, and the choices you have. It applies to everyone, and includes specific sections for users in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, and Switzerland (§9.1) and the United States (§9.2). We collect as little as possible and never sell your data.

1. Who we are

BrainQueue is operated by the BrainQueue team, the “data controller” for your information. For any privacy question or request, contact husseine.keita@gmail.com.

2. What we collect

  • Account information. When you sign in (Google, GitHub, Microsoft, Apple, or an email magic link), we receive your email address and, where the provider supplies it, your name and profile picture.
  • Your tasks. Titles, notes, categories, priority scores, and timestamps you create, stored so they sync across your devices.
  • Calendar events you choose to add. When you click “Add to calendar”, we create the single event you configured in your Google or Microsoft calendar. See §5.
  • Optional AI key. The “Brain Dump” feature can use your own Anthropic API key. If you enter one, it is stored only in your browser and sent directly from your browser to Anthropic. We never receive, store, or access it.
  • Technical data. Standard request logs (e.g. IP address, browser type) handled by our hosting providers for security and reliability.

We do not collect payment information, special-category/sensitive data, or use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

3. How we use your data

  • To provide the service: authenticate you, store and sync your tasks, and create calendar events you request.
  • To keep the service secure and working (abuse prevention, debugging, backups).
  • To send essential service messages (such as a sign-in magic link).

4. Legal bases for processing (EEA / UK / Switzerland)

Where the GDPR or UK GDPR applies, we rely on:

  • Performance of a contract — to give you the account and features you ask for (storing/syncing your tasks, creating calendar events).
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the service secure, reliable, and free from abuse, balanced against your rights.
  • Consent — for any optional processing (e.g. connecting calendar access). You can withdraw consent at any time, without affecting prior processing.
  • Legal obligation — where we must retain limited records to comply with law.

5. Google & Microsoft user data — calendar access

If you sign in with Google and use the calendar feature, BrainQueue requests the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.events scope, used for one purpose only: to create the specific event you explicitly choose to add from a task. We do not read your existing events, do not store your calendar data on our servers, and do not share it.

BrainQueue’s use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

The same principle applies to Microsoft: the Calendars.ReadWrite permission is used solely to create events you ask us to add.

6. Who processes your data

We rely on a small number of trusted “processors”/“service providers”:

  • Supabase — authentication and database hosting. Every task row is protected by row-level security, so only your authenticated account can read or write it.
  • Vercel — hosting of the web application.
  • Google / Microsoft / GitHub / Apple — sign-in and (Google/Microsoft) calendar event creation.
  • Anthropic — only when you use Brain Dump, and only with your own API key, sent from your browser.

7. International data transfers

Our providers may process data in countries other than yours, including the United States. Where we transfer personal data out of the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (and the UK Addendum) or an adequacy decision. You can request more detail using the contact below.

8. Data retention & deletion

  • Your tasks are kept until you delete them or close your account.
  • You can delete any task at any time inside the app.
  • To delete your entire account and associated data, email husseine.keita@gmail.com. We delete account data within 30 days, except where limited records must be retained to comply with law.

9. Your privacy rights

9.1 EEA, UK & Switzerland

You have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, and port your personal data, to object to processing based on legitimate interests, and to withdraw consent. To exercise these, contact us at the email below; we respond within one month. You may also lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (for example, the CNIL in France or the ICO in the UK), though we’d appreciate the chance to resolve it first.

9.2 United States (California & other states)

If you are a US resident, you may have the right to know/access the personal information we hold, delete it, correct it, and obtain a portable copy. Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) and similar laws in states such as Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and others, you also have the right to opt out of the “sale” or “sharing” of personal information.

We do not sell or share your personal information, and we do not process it for cross-context behavioural advertising. We do not knowingly process the data of anyone under 16 for such purposes.

Categories collected (CCPA): identifiers (email, name, account ID), your user-generated content (tasks), and limited internet/network activity (logs). We use these only for the purposes in §3. You have a right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your rights. To make a request, email us; we may need to verify your identity against your account.

10. Cookies & local storage

We use your browser’s local storage to keep you signed in and to cache your tasks for fast/offline loading. These are essential to the app’s function; we do not use advertising or analytics cookies.

11. Children

BrainQueue is not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect their data.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. We will revise the “Last updated” date above and, for material changes, provide a more prominent notice.

Questions or requests: husseine.keita@gmail.com
